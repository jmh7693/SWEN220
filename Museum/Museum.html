Museum Model

You are to complete the model of a simple museum that loans some of its collection (at a hefty price) to individual and corporate clients. The model uses the predefined signatures and relations in the file Museum.als

The signatures, relations are documented, as are the facts, predicates and assertions you are to complete. If you have any questions about interpreting what is required, ask your instructor.

You are NOT to:

  • Add or modify any signatures or relations (fields),
  • Add any of your own facts, predicates, or assertions.
  • Edit anything other than the bodies of facts, predicates and assertions that are clearly marked as being your responsibility.

Brief Model Description

The signatures in the model are:

  • Picture - pictures in the museum's collection.
  • Client - individuals and corporations that can have pictures on loaned, and can express the desire for other pictures.
  • The one and only Museum, that tracks all the pictures on display, including some in the permanent collection that can never be loaned.

The facts are designed to ensure a consistent state, which means (among other things):

  • Pictures in the permanent collection are always on display.
  • All pictures are either on display or on loan to a client; no picture can be simultaneously on display and on loan.
  • No two clients can have the same picture loaned to them.
  • If a client desires a picture, it must be on loan (otherwise the museum could loan the picture immediately to the client).
  • No client can desire a picture that the client already has on loan.
